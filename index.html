About Me

I was born and raised in Athens, GA, and after high school I soon moved to Atlanta to attend Georgia Tech. While I have always had an appreciation for new technologies, I was at Tech when I built my first desktop and computers became a bigger hobby (and money pit). After graduating with a degree in business, I eventually got a job in Norcross working to price maintenance contracts for enterprise hardware such as servers and networking devices. My experiance building my own computer helped me land the job, as servers are made up from the same general components, and that helped speed up my learning curve. Four years later, I decided I was bored enough in my work to try something new and GT Coding Boot Camp seemed like a good way to get a fresh start in an area of interest. While I have had decent knowledge of the hardware side of things, software is where the most growth and sustainability in the work place is happening, and also seems like more fun. In my spare time I like walking my dog, watching NBA and NFL religiously, video games, and spending way too much time on my computer.
